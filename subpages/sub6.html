Help over time for boy and girls







The feature of help refers to friends’ acts of providing assistance and resources on tangible matters such as schoolwork and activity planning. Instrumental support from friends may be especially beneficial to minority adolescents who have access to fewer resources. Little is known about how boys and girls may differ in their tendency to provide help to friends.

At T1 and T2, girls are more likely to rate their friends as very frequent helpers by giving scores of 14 or 15 (around 25% and T1 and T2) than boys (around 7% at T1, 11% at T2). However, at T3, the percentage of girls that gave their friends such high ratings decreased (around 15%) and was similar with the percentage of boys (13%). Most participants reported medium to medium-high levels of help in their friendships, reflecting in around 50% of the scores being between 9-13 at all three time points.
