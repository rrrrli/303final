Friendship and friendship quality in adolescence




Adolescence is an important time period to form deep and close friendships. Friends who are close tend to have affection for each other, help each other, and spend time with one another.

Having high-quality close friendships is important for adolescentsâ€™ healthy development, as these friendships can increase positive emotions and reduce depressive symptoms.

Although we know that having high quality friendships is important in adolescence and that adolescents tend to have higher friendship quality than children, little research has been done on how friendship quality changes over time, or whether the pattern of change in friendship quality over time may be different for boys and girls.

Our data




Our study contains a group of adolescents (n = 117) who participated in our study for three times.

At Time 1 (T1), they are around 12 years old. They are 14 years old on average when they return for the Time 2 (T2) study, and 16 years old at Time 3 (T3) of the study. We have roughly the same number of boys and girls in our sample.

At all three time points, our participants filled out a Likert-scale measure named Friendship Quality Questionnaire, which assesses their friendship quality with a best or close friend. Features of friendship




A friendship can have both positive and negative features. Friendships with a lot of positive features and few negative features are considered to be high-quality.
Research has identified several different positive features that are indicative of friendship quality. This project aims to map out how do these specific features of friendship change throughout adolescence for boys and girls with some interactive visualizations. The features are: validation, intimate exchange, companionship, help, and conflict resolution.
