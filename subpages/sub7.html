Conflict resolution over time for boy and girls







As adolescent friendships grow closer, conflict can become more often and inevitable. Conflicts are normal interpersonal events, and may not equate with aggression or produce serious harm to the relationship. Adaptive conflict resolution, which is the ability to resolve conflict in a timely and effective manner, is an essential task in close friendships. Little is known about whether boys or girls tend to engage in better conflict resolution. However, research has shown that boys and girls may use different conflict resolution strategies.

It seems that at all three time points, most adolescents exhibit medium-high and high levels of conflict resolution (scores 10-15). For girls at T1, the most commonly reported score was 12 (22%). After a few years, the most common score was 15 (T2, 22%) and 14 (T3, 22%). For boys, the most commonly reported scores centered around 11-13 at all time points.
